Energy and Transportation

What is Energy?

The simplest definition of energy is "the ability to do work". Energy is how things change and move. It's everywhere around us and takes all sorts of forms. It takes energy to cook food, to drive to school, and to jump in the air.

Different forms of Energy are:-


Solar energy

Hydro energy

Wind energy

Ways to conserve energy:

1. Turn off the fan when you leave a room.

2. Close your drapes or drop your window shades during the day.

3. Wash your clothes in cold water.

4. Wrap or cover foods and drinks in the refrigerator.

5. Unplug your appliances before you go to bed. Even while your devices are turned off, they can suck electricity from your outlet.

6. Wash your dishes by hands.

7. Turn on your ceiling fans.

8. Turn the lights off in unoccupied rooms.

9. Shut your computer down when you are done with it.

What is Transportation?

Transportation, the movement of goods and persons from place to place and the various means by which such movement is accomplished.


Different types of transportation:

1. Land Transportation.

2. Marine Transportation.

3. Air Transportation.

Some ways to avoid accidents are:

1. Drive in the prescribed speed limits on the various roads.

2. Always remember that “Speed thrills but kills”.

3. Always put on helmets, seat belts and other safety equipments before driving a bicycle/ motor cycle/vehicle.

4. Do not drink and drive.

5. Never use mobile phones or ear phones while driving.

6. Know the traffic signs, signals, lights and traffic safety rules before you hit the road.

7. Always remember that “Road safety rules are best tools to avoid accidents”.

8. Do not drive for long hours in a stretch.

9. Have a proper beaks after every 2 hours of continuous driving.

What is carbon footprint?

Greenhouse gases are emitted through the production and consumption of goods and services. Carbon footprint is a concept used to quantify the impact of an activity, a person or a country on climate change.


How can we reduce carbon footprint?

1. Consume local and seasonal products (forget strawberries in winter)

2. Limit meat consumption, especially beef,Select fish from sustainable fishing.

3. Bring reusable shopping bags and avoid products with excessive plastic packaging.

4. Make sure to buy only what you need, to avoid waste.

5.Buy reusable bags and avoid products with plastic.
